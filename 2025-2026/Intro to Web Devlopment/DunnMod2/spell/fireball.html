Spell Gremoir Module 2

Conjuration of the Shadow and Bloom

Casting Time: 6–10 Seconds

The Fireball spell is the chef’s flambé of the battlefield — quick, hot, and impossible to ignore. Instead of long preparation, this version uses the simplest spark, fuel, and willpower, compressed into one breath. In the space of a heartbeat, flame condenses into a sphere and hurtles outward, carrying the caster’s intent to sear and scatter foes. It is volatile, dramatic, and beloved by nearly every magic-user for its sheer impact.

Ingredients

  • A snap of your fingers or strike of flint (the spark)
  • A lungful of air and focused exhale (the fuel)
  • A pinch of ash or coal dust kept on your person.
  • Both hands cupped together, ready to shape the flame

Instructions

  1. Strike the spark — with fingers, flint, or sheer will.
  2. Exhale sharply into your cupped hands, feeding the spark with breath.
  3. Drop the pinch of ash into the forming flame, binding it into substance.
  4. Compress the fire between your palms until it glows as a sphere.
  5. Release with a thrusting motion, hurling the fireball toward your target.
