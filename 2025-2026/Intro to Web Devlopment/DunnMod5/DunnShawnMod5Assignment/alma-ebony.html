Alma Pinherst

Ebony Odogaron

Before the banners and broadcast lights, Alma Pinherst served as a field medic in the border wilds, learning to steady her pulse while the world splintered around her. She met the Ebony Odogaron in the Old Vale after a collapse, its hide torn, its breath ragged; instead of striking, she knelt and stitched, and a wary truce became a vow. Alma’s training favors restraint over spectacle: heart-rate sync, soft-step drills, and silent cues tapped against the hilt of her blade. In combat she maps the arena like a clinic, isolating variables, forcing resets, and cutting only when the line is clean. The Odogaron mirrors her discipline—no wasted motion, no mercy for chaos—its bursts of speed arriving like a surgeon’s final suture. Their record is built on precision wins against tail-whip bruisers and aerial divers, where timing, not force, decides the match. Off the sand, Alma mentors rookies in wound care and ethical handling, insisting that power without empathy is just another injury waiting to happen. Together they embody a quiet doctrine: control the breath, control the cut, control the hunt.
